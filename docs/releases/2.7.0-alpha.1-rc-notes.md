# Sales Appointment Capture 2.7.0-alpha.1 RC Notes

## Purpose

This release candidate consolidates the approved appointment workflow and prepares it for physical iPhone acceptance testing. It does not constitute production approval.

## Included changes

- Production staff seed: Blake Duffield, Joe Villiers-Dunn, Josh Robinson, Mike Enderby, Garry Lewis, Natalie Simmich, and Sam Roberts.
- Contract Due Date with an explicit mutually exclusive `To Be Confirmed` choice and final-generation validation.
- Mobile-safe conveyancer selector with B.O.S.S Conveyancing, Natalie to Confirm, and an optional custom value.
- Prepared email with approved recipients, appointment details, Contract Due Date, conditional next appointment, and staff sign-off.
- One complete appointment package containing a combined PDF and document ZIP, with revision-aware reuse and invalidation.
- A deliberate package-ready handover: Share Package, Save Combined PDF, Save ZIP, and Prepare Email.
- Responsive workspace and package-ready layouts with 44px minimum action targets.
- Service-worker cache `v2.7.0-alpha.20`.

## RC Hotfix 1 — Contract Due Date visibility

- Promoted Contract Due Date from an anonymous grid cell to an explicit, labelled form group with a restrained visual marker so its native date input and `To Be Confirmed` choice remain prominent at mobile and desktop widths.
- Preserved the approved date/TBC mutual exclusion, validation, draft, email, PDF, and package-invalidation behaviour.
- Physical-device retesting remains required; automated visibility does not approve native Safari rendering.

## RC Hotfix 2 — Contract Due Date placement

- Moved the single shared Contract Due Date group directly below the relevant Next Appointment Date: EOI for in-person appointments and Client Review for Zoom appointments.
- When optional in-person EOI details are hidden, Contract Due Date remains visible in the EOI section rather than inheriting the hidden state.
- No Contract Due Date business, draft, validation, email, PDF, package, or accessibility behaviour changed.
- Cache advances to `v2.7.0-alpha.18`; focused physical placement retesting remains required.

## RC Hotfix 3 — Sale Details finance layout

- In-person Sale Details now presents separate Client 1 and participating Client 2 finance percentages, followed by one grouped EOI Next Appointment area and Contract Due Date beside it on wide screens.
- Mobile stacks finance controls, then the complete date/time group, then Contract Due Date. Zoom retains its Client Review Next Appointment Date beside Contract Due Date and exposes no EOI finance or time controls.
- Legacy single-finance drafts map valid values to Client 1 only. The standard EOI PDF has one approved finance location, so it receives Client 1; no verified Client 2 finance target exists and PDF geometry remains unchanged.
- Cache advances to `v2.7.0-alpha.19`; the focused physical-device layout and finance retest remains NOT TESTED.

## RC hotfix — Zoom timeline and whiteboard

- Restored the existing eight-step Zoom timeline to its intended horizontal flex presentation without changing in-person navigation.
- Added a container-scoped whiteboard resize observer so the canvas receives non-zero backing dimensions when the hidden Zoom workspace is revealed. The observer and resize listener are cleaned up on page lifecycle exit.
- Cache advances to `v2.7.0-alpha.20`; focused physical confirmation remains NOT TESTED.

## Verification summary

- Full Node/browser regression suite and authoritative Phase 5 suite are required to pass before the RC is handed to a device tester.
- Automated viewport coverage includes 320×568, 375×667, 390×844, 393×852, 414×896, 844×390, 412×915, 1024×768, 1280×800, 1440×900, and 1920×1080.
- Automated service-worker coverage includes fresh install, upgrade from `v2.7.0-alpha.15`, cache cleanup, offline reload, offline package generation, offline saves, and prepared-email creation.

## Known limitations

- Real iPhone software keyboard, native date picker, Safari downloads/Files integration, native Share sheet, mail composer, safe-area behavior, rotation, installed-PWA chrome, and installed-PWA cache upgrade require physical-device verification.
- A LAN HTTP URL is not a trusted secure context on iPhone; service-worker and native file-sharing results from that URL cannot approve those secure-context behaviours.
- Production deployment remains blocked until the physical-device checklist is completed and reviewed.
